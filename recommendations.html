Know Before You Go

A few practical notes that make exploring Zona Romantica feel easier, safer, and more relaxed.

Local Tips
A Few Helpful Basics
Very walkable
Many guests will not need a car at all once they are settled in.
⚠️Uneven sidewalks and high curbs
Sidewalks in Zona Romantica can be surprisingly uneven, narrow, and elevated. Please watch your footing carefully, especially at night, after drinks, or on unfamiliar streets.
Cash helps
Pesos are useful for tips, taxis, smaller shops, and casual food stops.
Neighborhood Rhythm
The Feel Of The Neighborhood
Street food can be great
Some of the best casual meals are taco stands and market stops, especially in the evening.

This area can stay lively into the night, so light sleepers may appreciate earplugs.
Local charm is part of the draw
Puerto Vallarta blends polished hospitality with a lived-in neighborhood feel, which is part of what makes it special.

Transportation And Deliveries

A few practical notes that make getting around Puerto Vallarta much easier once you are out and exploring.

Getting Around
Uber is usually easiest
For most rides around town, Uber is still the easiest option because pricing is set in the app and payment is handled automatically. Basic Uber can be very basic here, so if you want a slightly more comfortable ride, Uber Black is often worth trying since the price difference is usually small, just do not expect a true luxury car every time.
Taxis still work well
Taxis are easy to find and can be very convenient for short hops, but it is smart to confirm the fare before the ride starts. Many drivers prefer cash, and not all will take cards, so pesos are helpful to have on hand.
Food delivery
Uber Eats and Rappi are both reliable for an easy night in. If you are settling in after travel day, they are often the fastest route to dinner without needing to think too much about it.
